Company History

First Generation Video Games:

The 1940s was the beginning of an era. SEGA was one of the first mass produced video game counsels. The first video game counsel that plugged into the TV was the Magnavox Odyssey. Many of the companies failed due to how expensive their systems were. The first video game/arcade game was Pong which was founded by the Atari company. Many of the big video game companies of today started out with those simple games. A notable company was Nintendo , a company that originated in Japan. These game consoles showed black and white pictures.

Second Generation Video Games:

Video games started to become more advanced for their time; many companies started making it so their counsel could have interchangeable games and multi-colored outputs. These games were known as cartridges. This time was known as the Golden Age. With the arrival of popular games such as Pacman, Donkey Kong, and Mario, the tides were changed for the companies and they started getting more money. Later on, the graphics started to improve too and in the 1990, companies started to make their games in 3D. Although the 3D graphics were good to today's standards, back then, they were all of the rave.

Third Generation Video Games:

The newest video games were 8-bit video games. This meant that the processing power of their console was larger than their predecessors. Nintendo was the first company to release an 8-bit system which was known as the Nintendo Entertainment System NES. This marked the shift of the video game industry from the US to Japan.

Fouth Generation Video Games:

After the invention of 16-Bit video game consoles, 8-Bit games lost popularity. Nintendo Came out with the Super Nintendo Entertainment System SNES. CD-ROMs first appeared in this era and soon took over the video game industry, leaving the cartridges in the dust. The discontinuation of the SNES proved to be the end of this generation of video game consoles. The introduction of CD helped revolutionize the world of PC Gaming.

Fifth Generation Video Games:

This generation is known as the 32-bit era, the 64-bit era and the 3D era . The Playstation was the popular CD playing video game console. It used a flashdrive-like device to save played games. The Nintendo 64 was released, being one of the few cartridge playing counsels. Several handheld games were also released, including the GameBoy Color, a Nintendo product. With the discontinuation of the Playstation 1, marked the end of this era.

Sixth Generation Video Games:

The top producers of the 128-bit era were Sony and Microsoft. The Playstation 2 started using DVDs for their games, but they were backwards compatable with their CDs from the PS One. It included an ethernet adapter to connect to the internet. The GameCube was the mini-DVD playing consoles released by Nintendo. It was less popular than the other two due to the limited space for games and the fact that it was not backwards compatable with its predecessors. The Game Boy Advanced was a handheld game made by Nintendo. The xBox was released by Microsoft, and used a Intel Pentium 3 CPU. The games were programmed with DirectX making PC easily compatable with the xBox. One of the popular games was Halo. When the Playstation 2 was discontinued in 2013, this generation was over.

Seventh Generation Video Games:

The two handheld video game consoles of this era were the Nintendo DS and the Sony Play Station Portable PSP. The Wii was developed by Nintendo and was one of the first consoles that used motion sensors and special remotes and platforms to make playing games more realistic and engaging. Microsoft followed suit and developed the xBox 360 and the motion detector system known as Kinect which does not use controls.

Eight Generation Video Games

The currend eras of video games is Eight Generation. Sony developed the Playstation 3, which had its games on Blu-Rays and connected to the internet with WiFi. They too developed movement oriented controlers for their games. Nintendo is leading in the handheld game market with their DS game line. The most current one is the 3DS XL . They also developed the Wii U which controller that is Touch Screen, making it possible to play a game without the use of the TV. The newest Microsoft game console is the xBox One , but the new features of Windows 10 make it possible to play the games on a computer, so long as they are xBox Live games. Sony has the Playstation 4 and the PS Vita. These consoles use x86-64 arcitecture.
