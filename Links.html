Company History

Here is a list of three related companies.

  • Gamefly: Founded in 2002 by Sean Spector, Jung Suh, and Toby Lenk Gamefly is an online company allowing users to pay per month, allowing them to rent a certain amount of games at a time, but the ammount of games rented per month is unlimited. Growing fast since then, this company has 334,000 subscribers as of September 30, 2009. The GameFly website and Mobile App make it so that customers can buy, rent, and play all from the palm of their hand.
  • Gamestop: Gamestop's roots were tied to the company Babbage's, a small software retailer that started in Dallas, Texas. After several mergers, Babbage's was aquired around 2000 by Funco, Inc., and renamed Gamestop. Gamestop is a Chain retailer offering new & used video games, plus consoles, controllers & related gear.
  • Gamespot:Launched on May 1, 1996, by Pete Deemer, Vince Broady and Jon Epstein, GameSpot is a video gaming website that provides news, reviews, previews, downloads, and other information on certain video games. This outfit is owned by CBS Interactive.
